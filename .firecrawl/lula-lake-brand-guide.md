3 — Intro
1 Introduction
1.1 BRAND BRIEF
Lula Lake Sound is a high-end, retreat-style recording studio at the edge of Lookout
Mountain, just beyond the protected landscape of Lula Lake. The studio is the result
of Jeff Grizzard and Molly Waters’ shared passion for music, nature, and intentional
design—a place built for artists who need room to slow down and create.
The environment reflects a deep respect for the natural world and the creative
process alike. Clean lines, raw textures, and quiet hospitality offer a rare kind of
clarity. An adjoining organic farm carries that ethos even further, grounding the space
in rhythm, restoration, and care.
There’s no hard sell here—just a studio with nothing to prove and everything to offer.
Its presence spreads quietly, from artist to artist, rooted in experience rather than
exposure. Think Wilco, Father John Misty, and the thoughtful textures of Big Thief or
Fleet Foxes. Not inspirations to replicate, but signals that this is a place where music
breathes a little deeper.

4 — Intro
1.2 PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT
This brand guide exists to protect the understated yet strong essence of Lula Lake
Sound. It offers a simple framework for expressing the studio’s identity across design,
photography, and materials. Every element here is meant to support the feel of the
physical space: calm, grounded, and thoughtfully made. This isn’t a marketing tool. It’s
a way to stay consistent without losing the subtlety that defines this place.
Whether you’re designing an album insert, capturing the space in photos, or helping
artists share their experience on socials, this guide is here to keep things aligned with
the studio’s aesthetics.

11 — Wordmark
2.4 WORDMARK
The wordmark features the name “Lula Lake Sound” in a single horizontal line, set in Haettenschweiler,
a condensed sans-serif typeface chosen specifically for its bold stance and sculptural presence. While it
complements the studio’s overall brand tone, it is not used for headlines or body copy elsewhere.
The kerning has been intentionally adjusted to create a sense of rhythm and weight—giving the mark a
grounded, architectural feel that mirrors the space itself. It should never be recreated or altered manually;
always use the provided artwork files.
This version is the most minimal expression of the identity—clean, typographic, and unembellished. It’s
ideal for contexts where space is limited, the brand is already established, or where the graphic triangle
would feel overly formal. Common uses include signage, footers, file labels, digital templates, or equipment
branding.
The wordmark is available in a range of solid color options that align with the broader brand palette as well
as a limited palette of textured variation.

2.5 COLOR VARIATIONS
Lula Lake Sound’s logo system is offered in a curated set of color variations to ensure clarity, tone, and
flexibility across mediums. These include Washed Black, Ivory, and select tonal options such as Rust, Sage,

aligned with the space itself. Color is a delicate constellation. When in doubt, lead with restraint.

13 — Use Standards
2.6 LOGO USE STANADARDS
Clear Space
To preserve the clarity and impact of the Lula
Lake Sound identity, each logo variation requires
adequate space around it, free from competing
elements like text, imagery, or other graphics.
Primary Logo & Graphic Logo
For these symbol-based marks, maintain
a minimum clear space equal to half the
height of the triangle on all sides. This
ensures the logo remains legible and
grounded without being overcrowded or
ornamental.
Stacked Logo & Wordmark
While these marks are typographic, they
still require visual breathing room. Allow
for comfortable margins to preserve their
clarity and tone—especially when used
in close proximity to other text or layout
elements.
Across all formats, the logo should feel deliberate
and well-placed. Let it hold space with ease,
never forced into corners or clutter.

14 — Use Standards
Minimum Size
To ensure legibility and brand integrity, each logo
variation must not be scaled below its minimum
size threshold. At smaller sizes, fine details
or letterforms can become unclear or visually
imbalanced.
Primary Logo
Minimum width: 1.25 inches (90 px)
This size maintains clarity of both the
triangle and the wordmark when printed or
used digitally.
Graphic Logo
Minimum width: .5 inches (36 px)
Because it contains no small text, the
icon can be used at smaller scales—such
as corner tags, footers, or social media
overlays.
Stacked Logo
Minimum width: .75 inches (54 px)
Avoid shrinking the stacked composition
too far, as the wordmark can begin to crowd
vertically.
Wordmark
Minimum width: 1.25 inches (90 px)
Maintain enough space for the letterforms
to feel distinct and deliberate, especially in
print or low-resolution digital contexts.
File format note:
For applications near the minimum size
threshold—especially in print—use vector
formats (AI, EPS) whenever possible to maintain
crisp edges and consistent line weight. Raster
formats (like PNG) are suitable for digital use but
may lose sharpness at small sizes.

15 — Use Standards
Incorrect Usage
The Lula Lake Sound logo system is carefully
constructed. Alterations—however well-
meaning—can compromise its clarity, tone, and
purpose. Always use the provided artwork files
and avoid the following:
• Do not stretch, squash, or rotate the logo.
• Do not change the logo colors outside the
approved palette.
• Do not apply drop shadows, bevels,
gradients, or other effects.
• Do not place the logo on backgrounds that
reduce legibility.
• Do not recreate the wordmark or typeset it
manually.
• Do not combine elements from different
logos.
The Lula Lake Sound identity works best when
it’s left to speak quietly and clearly. Let it do its
job without embellishment.

27 — Photograhpy
5.3 PHOTOGRAPHY
Photography at Lula Lake Sound should
reflect the space’s natural atmosphere:
expressive, raw, and grounded in natural
light. The desired look draws inspiration
from 35mm film aesthetics—naturally
lit scenes, gentle contrast, and a sense of
warmth and grain without artificial polish.
Images may be captured digitally but
should be treated with low to mid
saturation, a subtle layer of film-quality
grain, and an overall sense of restraint.
The tone should feel present, unhurried,
and real—an extension of the space itself.
For more graphic applications,
photography can be layered with the
brand’s texture system or Chladni
patterns to add mood and dimension.
These treatments should remain subtle—
used to enhance the environment, not
dominate it.
Stylistic Notes:
• Favor natural light and minimal post-
processing.
• Let shadows, texture, and human
presence come through.
• Avoid overly saturated or high-
contrast edits.
• Maintain a consistent visual rhythm
across uses.
